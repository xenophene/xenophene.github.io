International A3-17 U.N. PRESSED TO ACT IN YUGOSLAVIA

Worried that a cease-fire could break down, a special United Nations envoy recommended that a larger-than-expected peacekeeping force be deployed in Yugoslavia, Western diplomats said. A1 ELDER REBUKES JAPANESE

The man described as the godfather of Japanese politics chided some of his country's leaders, saying that their criticisms of American work habits had seriously worsened relations with the United States. A1

UKRAINE'S UNOFFICIAL CURRENCY

By hastily transforming coupons into cash, the Ukrainian Government has in effect given birth to a new national currency. The move makes the growing separation of the Ukrainian and Russian economies seem impossible to reverse. A1 COURTING NEIGHBORS OF IRAN

On the second day of a whirlwind tour, Secretary of State Baker said Turkmenistan and Azerbaijan had met his conditions for diplomatic relations. Leaders of both former Soviet republics pledged to observe democratic principles. A12 PAKISTAN OPENS FIRE IN KASHMIR

Alarmed by the possibility of a conflict with India, Pakistan violently halted a march by Kashmiri separatists who were trying to cross the border into the Indian-controlled portion of Kashmir. A3 CANADA REINS IN BORDER SHOPPERS

Canada's Government took action to try to curb the billions of dollars that its citizens spend shopping for goods in the United States. Steps include a new $5 handling fee on packages mailed to Canada. A10 VIOLATING 2 CHINESE TABOOS

Of all the criminal cases in China involving democracy advocates, perhaps the most mysterious concerns an official who was sentenced to four years in prison for engaging in an overly dynamic sex life. A8 LIBYAN OVERTURE TO FRANCE

Libya said it was ready to cooperate with French investigations of charges that its agents blew up a French airliner in 1989. But Libya seems to be resisting demands to turn over two agents in the bombing of a Pan Am flight. A9 Madrid Journal: Acknowledging the painful side to 1492. A4 With Soviet demise, Swedish waters are quiet. A13 National A18-25, B13-17 BILL CLINTON AND THE DRAFT

In an effort to prevent further damage to his Presidential campaign, Governor Clinton got the jump on an ABC News report by releasing a letter, written by him 22 years ago, in which he thanked an Army officer for "saving me from the draft" and apologized for deceiving the officer about his intention to join the R.O.T.C. Democrats on the House Ways and Means Committee sent a package of the President's tax proposals to the floor, where they expect it to die. Then they ended a bitter open meeting with Republicans and went behind closed doors to begin writing their own alternative. A1 SPACE AGENCY'S CHIEF IS QUITTING

After a long, often losing battle with the White House over the direction of the nation's space program, Richard Truly announced his resignation as NASA's Administrator. A1 FROM DROUGHT TO DELUGE

For most of the last decade, Southern Californians have struggled through drought. This week they have been forced to cope with the opposite problem: rain storms that have brought the Los Angeles area its worst flooding in more than half a century. A18 THE BALLOON BRIGADE

They call themselves helium-heads and air-heads because helium and hot air make balloons fly. For them lighter-than-air flight is something akin to love, and they have flocked to Akron, Ohio, to offer assistance to the crew of Earthwinds, the most complex long-distance balloon ever built, which is scheduled to lift off sometime before dawn tomorrow for a flight around the world.
