International 2-5 ISRAELI FORCES PULL BACK

Israeli forces withdrew from two villages in southern Lebanon, ending a 24-hour thrust against Shiite Muslim guerrillas. After the pullout, Shiite gunmen fired fresh barrages of rockets at Israeli border villages, killing a 5-year-old girl. 1

SHEIK'S DEATH WAS LONG PLOTTED

Israel's decision to kill the Lebanese Party of God leader, Sheik Abbas Musawi, was made months ago. Israeli officials said they had postponed the slaying to avoid interfering with efforts to free Western hostages and get word on missing Israeli servicemen. 1 Israel and the United States failed to agree on loan guarantees. 4 SAUDIS WARM TO CENTRAL ASIA

In a move intended to blunt Iranian influence in Central Asia, Saudi Arabia has established diplomatic ties with the former Soviet republic of Uzbekistan. 4 U.S. EASES PENALTIES ON CHINA

The Bush Administration lifted sanctions barring high-technology sales to China after accepting its promise to abide by restrictions on the sale of missiles and missile technology. 1 U.N. SENDING TROOPS TO CROATIA

The United Nations Security Council voted unanimously to start dispatching peacekeepers to Yugoslavia to monitor the cease-fire there and protect Croatia's Serbian minority. 3 JAPAN DEFENDS DEPORTATION

Rejecting criticism, Japan said it acted properly last year in deporting a Chinese refugee seeking political asylum -- even though she was jailed upon her return to China. 3 YES OR NO, SOUTH AFRICAN ASKS

By calling a vote, South Africa's President is trying to jolt the white minority into confronting the alternative offered by the right-wing Conservative Party, which opposes his moves to abandon apartheid. 3 U.S. BERATES SUDAN ON REFUGEES

The Sudanese Government has driven 400,000 displaced people out of shelters and into the desert, a United States official said. He called the evictions, carried out at gunpoint, "a death sentence." 5 5,000-YEAR-OLD 'ICEMAN'

Carbon dating tests show that the well-preserved body of a prehistoric human hunter found last year in an Alpine glacier is 5,000 to 5,500 years old, scientists reported. 5 CAN SMOG BE BLOWN AWAY?

Mexico City Journal: On orders from the Mayor, researchers study a plan to blow the city's infamous smog away with giant fans. 2 National 6-9 QUAYLE WOOS CONSERVATIVES

Vice President Quayle sought to undermine Patrick Buchanan's bid for the Presidency by telling Republican conservatives that working against President Bush would hurt the party. 1 A BIT OF MOMENTUM FOR TSONGAS

Paul Tsongas gained substantial support around the nation with his victory in the New Hampshire primary, pulling close to Gov. Bill Clinton, but he remained largely unknown in the latest New York Times/CBS News poll. 1 BUSH IGNORES BUCHANAN, MOSTLY

At a Republican leadership conference, President Bush attacked familiar issues like tax increases and socialized medicine, but only indirectly assailed the nettlesome foe ensconced in a hotel a block away. 9 Don't draft me for President, Governor Cuomo said. 8 C.I.A. TO OPEN ARCHIVES

The director of the Central Intelligence Agency said he would speed up the declassification of many agency archives, including some on the assassination of President John F. Kennedy and the Bay of Pigs invasion. 9 Washington Talk: The Persian Gulf war shapes a budget battle. 9 The Maine caucuses promised to be anything but predictable. 8 A VOTE TO HELP COLLEGE STUDENTS

The Senate voted to make more middle-class families eligible for college grants and increased the maximum annual grant to $3,600. 6 ANGER OVER IMPLANT LIMITS

Plastic surgeons expressed confusion and anger over an expert panel's recommendation of tight restrictions on the use of silicone-gel breast implants. 6 BRUTALITY TRIAL IN QUIET CITY

Simi Valley Journal: A city that grew up fast but remained "kind of off the beaten path" sat uncomfortably in the spotlight as the unlikely site of the Rodney King beating trial. 6 An inquiry cast doubt on part of the Huey Long legend. 6 Boston's Mayor mourned a slain Japanese university chief. 7 Religion notes: Church, state and the I.R.S. 7 Metropolitan Digest, 25 CITY HALL ACROSS THE RIVER

On Monday morning, Mayor Dinkins and his top aides will transport the nerve center of New York City government to Brooklyn Borough Hall for a week of political theater aimed at putting the Mayor of all of the people in closer contact with some of them. 1 Business Digest 37 Sports 30-36 NO HOCKEY GOLD FOR U.S.
