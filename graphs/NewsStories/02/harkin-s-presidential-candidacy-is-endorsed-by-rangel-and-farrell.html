Harkin's Presidential Candidacy Is Endorsed by Rangel and Farrell

By DENNIS HEVESI
Published: February 02, 1992
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print

The Presidential candidacy of Senator Tom Harkin of Iowa was endorsed yesterday by Representative Charles B. Rangel and Assemblyman Herman D. Farrell Jr., the chairman of the Manhattan Democratic organization.

At a news conference outside the Adam Clayton Powell State Office Building in Harlem, Mr. Rangel said Senator Harkin is the only candidate with "a clear vision for ending the economic misery that has resulted from 12 years of Reaganomics."

Later Mr. Rangel, from Manhattan, said he was supporting Mr. Harkin "because everyone says he can't win because he's too much of a Democrat."

"So I said to myself, 'Hey, let me take another look at him,' " Mr. Rangel continued. "I'm telling you that every reason that my Democratic friends give me as to why they cannot support him has reinforced my support of him. They say he's too much like Franklin Roosevelt, that the days of the New Deal are over. He represents the traditions of the Democratic Party." 'A New New Deal'

In a statement, Mr. Farrell praised Senator Harkin's commitment to the nation's cities. "Tom Harkin's economic plan is the best of all the candidates' to address the needs of the cities," Mr. Farrell said.

The Senator said he was "extremely proud" to receive the endorsement of Mr. Rangel and Mr. Farrell, as well as those of about 20 other New York Democrats who attended the news conference. He likened his positions to "a New New Deal," and asserted, "That's where I differ from the other Democrats.

"The message is," he said, "no more tax breaks for the rich, quit exporting our jobs, quit spending billions defending Europe and Japan, bring the money home, invest in America."

"Let's use the power of the Federal Government to bring hope and confidence to people," he continued, but "not through trickle-down" economics.

Mr. Harkin, 52 years, old, has been in the Senate since 1985. He was a member of the House of Representatives from 1975 to 1985.

Senator Harkin is one of five Democrats seeking their party's nomination. The others are former Gov. Edmund G. Brown Jr. of California, Gov. Bill Clinton of Arkansas, Senator Bob Kerrey of Nebraska and former Senator Paul E. Tsongas of Massachusetts.
